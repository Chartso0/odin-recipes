Sweet Potato Cheesecake

Description

This sweet potato cheesecake has a smooth, creamy texture with a buttery crumb crust

and delectable pecan praline topping. Perfect for a rich and flavorful fall or winter dessert.

  • 1 ¼ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • ⅞ cup white sugar
  • ⅓ cup sour cream
  • ¼ cup heavy whipping cream
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • ¼ cup butter for topping
  • ¼ cup heavy whipping cream for topping
  • 1 cup chopped pecans for topping

    • Directions

    1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C ).

    2. Mix graham cracker crumbs, 1/4 cup sugar, and 1/4 cup melted butter together in a bowl until combined.

      Press crumb mixture firmly and evenly across the bottom and slightly up the sides of a 9 ½-inch springform pan.

      Bake for 10 minutes; remove from oven and set aside. Keep the oven on.

    3. Beat cream cheese and 7/8 cup of white sugar until smooth.

      Mix in sour cream, 1/4 cup cream, and sweet potato puree. Beat in eggs one at a time, blending well after each. Pour filling into crust.

    4. Bake in the preheated oven until a tester inserted near the center comes out clean, about 1 hour (center may still be slightly jiggly).

      Turn off the oven. Let cake stand 1 hour in oven with door ajar.

    5. To make the praline topping: Combine brown sugar and 1/4 cup butter in a heavy small saucepan.

      Stir over low heat until sugar dissolves. Increase heat, and bring to a boil. Mix in 1/4 cup cream, then add pecans.

    6. Pour hot praline topping over cheesecake. Store leftover cheesecake in the refrigerator.

    7. Serve and enjoy!
